‚öΩ Overview

Football, also known as soccer, is the world's most popular sport, played between two teams of 11 players each. The goal is to score points by getting the ball into the opponent's net while following the rules of the game.

It is a fast-paced sport requiring agility, coordination, and teamwork. Played in backyards, streets, schools, and professional stadiums, football brings people together across the globe.

üìú Basic Rules

  • Objective: Score by getting the ball into the opponent's goal.
  • Game Duration:
    • 90 minutes (two halves of 45 minutes each).
    • Extra time and penalty shootouts in case of a draw (in knockout matches).
  • Scoring:
    • 1 Goal = 1 Point (only if the entire ball crosses the goal line).
  • Game Start:
    • Begins with a kickoff from the center of the field.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Violations

  • Handball: Touching the ball with hands (except the goalkeeper inside the penalty area).
  • Offside: An attacking player is ahead of the last defender when the ball is passed to them.
  • Fouls:
    • Tripping, pushing, or tackling an opponent unfairly.
    • Results in free kicks, penalty kicks, or yellow/red cards.
  • Corner Kick: Awarded if the defending team last touches the ball before it crosses their goal line.
  • Throw-In: Awarded if the ball completely crosses the sideline.

2

3

Defenders work to block shots and regain possession.

4

Players shoot the ball past the goalkeeper to score.

The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins.
