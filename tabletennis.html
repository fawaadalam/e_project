🏓 Overview

Table Tennis, also known as ping pong, is a fast-paced indoor sport played on a rectangular table divided by a net. Players use small paddles to hit a lightweight ball back and forth, aiming to score points by making the ball bounce twice on the opponent's side or forcing an error.

From casual home games to international championships, table tennis is loved worldwide for its quick reflex action and strategic gameplay.

Table Tennis: Quick Guide 🏓

🏟️ Setup & Equipment

  • Players: Singles (1v1) or Doubles (2v2)
  • Table: 9ft (2.74m) long, 5ft (1.525m) wide, 30 inches (76cm) high
  • Net: 6 inches (15.25cm) tall, stretched across the middle
  • Ball: 40mm in diameter, made of celluloid or plastic
  • Paddles: Rubber-coated wooden or composite rackets

📜 Basic Rules

Objective: Score points by winning rallies against your opponent.

Scoring:

  • A game is played to 11 points (win by 2).
  • A match is typically best of 5 or 7 games.

Serving:

  • The ball must be tossed at least 6 inches before being struck.
  • The serve alternates every 2 points (except at 10-10, where it changes after each point).

Winning a Point:

  • If the opponent fails to return the ball.
  • If the ball bounces twice on the opponent's side.
  • If the opponent hits the ball off the table without bouncing.

⚠️ Common Violations

  • Missed Serve: Failing to make the ball land on the opponent's side.
  • Net Violation: If the ball touches the net and does not land properly.
  • Illegal Shot: Striking the ball before it bounces on your side.

🎯 How to Play

1️⃣

Start with a Serve

Toss the ball and strike it diagonally to begin the rally.

2️⃣

Return the Ball Strategically

Use spin, speed, and placement to challenge your opponent.

3️⃣

Control the Pace

Mix fast smashes with slow spins to outplay your opponent.

4️⃣

Defend Against Attacks

Block or counter heavy smashes with quick reflexes.

5️⃣

Adapt and Outsmart

Read your opponent's strategy and adjust your game accordingly.

6️⃣

Win the Game!

Be the first to reach 11 points (with a 2-point lead) to secure victory.

Table Tennis is a game of lightning-fast reflexes, skillful strategy, and high-energy rallies. Whether played for fun or at a competitive level, every match is an electrifying test of precision and agility!
