🏸 Overview

Badminton is a fast-paced racket sport played between two players (singles) or two pairs (doubles). The objective is to score points by hitting the shuttlecock over the net and landing it in the opponent's court.

The game is known for its quick reflexes, powerful smashes, and strategic shot placements. Played in backyards, gyms, and international arenas, badminton is a thrilling test of skill and endurance.

Badminton: Quick Guide 🏸

🏟️ Setup & Equipment

  • Players: 1 vs. 1 (Singles) or 2 vs. 2 (Doubles)
  • Court: 13.4m x 6.1m (doubles) / 13.4m x 5.18m (singles)
  • Net Height: 1.55m at sides, 1.524m at center
  • Shuttlecock: Feather or synthetic
  • Rackets: Lightweight graphite/aluminum

📜 Basic Rules

  • Score points by landing shuttle in opponent's court
  • Rally scoring system (every rally = point)
  • First to 21 points wins (2 point lead needed)
  • Best of 3 games wins match
  • Serve diagonally from service court

⚠️ Common Violations

  • Service Fault: Improper serve technique
  • Double Hit: Multiple hits by same player
  • Net Touch: Player/racket contacts net
  • Out of Bounds: Shuttle lands outside lines

🎯 How to Play

1

Serve to Start the Rally

The game begins with a serve hit diagonally across the net into the opponent's service box.

2

Rally by Hitting the Shuttlecock

Players return the shuttlecock using smashes, drops, and clears to gain an advantage.

3

Move Strategically

Quick footwork is key to reaching the shuttlecock and controlling the rally.

4

Score Points

Winning points requires precise shot placement and deception.

5

Defend Effectively

Defending against smashes and quick returns is crucial for survival in rallies.

6

Win the Game!

First to 21 points with a 2-point lead wins the game.
